About

I’m a sophomore at NYU Stern in the Business, Technology & Entrepreneurship program, working toward a career in investment banking. My path here is unconventional: I built a comic book resale business generating $15K+ in revenue during high school, funded by three years working as a McDonald’s Crew Trainer — where I ranked #1 out of 50+ locations. Those earnings opened my first brokerage account and started a genuine obsession with how capital is deployed.

“I worked to fund a business. Then I used that business to fund my first trade. Then I got obsessed.”

At Stern, I’ve been a Real Estate PE & Credit Fellow at Project Destined (winning the Private Credit Cup), a Hedge Fund Fellow with JBiz focused on merger arbitrage and financial modeling, and led product strategy research for a BTE Design Sprint. I’m also active in the Stern Jewish Business Association and BTE Leadership Council.

My edge is thinking like an operator — because I’ve been one. Understanding the friction of running a real business translates directly into how I analyze companies.
